# Hook Campus Resources

This is curated demo guidance for Hook's campus-support chat. It is intentionally local, simple, and demo-safe.

## Study Spaces

- Perry-Castaneda Library: quiet floors, reservable study rooms, and long evening hours during most of the semester.
- Engineering Education and Research Center commons: strong choice for group work, whiteboards, and project collaboration.
- Student Union lounges: good for casual study, coffee meetings, and low-pressure work sessions between classes.
- If a student needs fewer distractions, recommend a quiet-floor library seat first and a reserved room only if they need group discussion.

## Tutoring and Academic Help

- Sanger Learning Center: tutoring, supplemental instruction, study-skills coaching, and exam-prep support.
- Office hours: best next step when the student is confused on one class, one concept, or one assignment.
- Writing support: recommend a campus writing center when the student needs help with essays, scholarship statements, or outreach drafts.
- If a student is struggling across multiple courses, suggest tutoring plus academic advising instead of trying to solve everything alone.

## Mental Health and Crisis Resources

- Counseling and Mental Health Center: first stop for counseling, short-term support, and mental-health referrals.
- 24/7 crisis support: if the student mentions self-harm, immediate danger, or a mental-health emergency, direct them to call or text 988 and contact campus crisis support immediately.
- Emergency safety: if someone is in immediate danger, tell them to call 911 right away.
- Hook should not diagnose. It should route students to urgent support, encourage reaching out to a trusted person, and keep the advice concrete.

## Food and Emergency Support

- Campus food pantry and basic-needs center: refer students here for short-term food support, emergency groceries, and practical assistance.
- Emergency aid office: good escalation path when a student has an urgent financial disruption affecting housing, food, or course participation.
- Dean of Students or student emergency services: route here for sudden crises, temporary hardship documentation, and support navigation.
- If the problem is urgent and practical, give the student one immediate place to contact today plus one backup office.

## Advising and Student Services

- Academic advising: best for degree planning, registration questions, major changes, probation concerns, and graduation timelines.
- Financial aid office: best for FAFSA/TASFA questions, aid package changes, verification, and scholarship coordination.
- Career services: use for resume review, internship search help, recruiting prep, and networking support.
- Disability and access services: route here when a student needs accommodations or support for classroom access.
- If the question mixes academics, aid, and scheduling, advise the student to start with academic advising and bring a written list of questions.
